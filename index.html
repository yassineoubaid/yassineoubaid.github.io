About

A research page, not a corporate brochure.

My work sits at the intersection of magnetism, structure, and electronic correlations. I am especially interested in unconventional superconductivity, low-dimensional and strongly correlated systems, and how multiple experimental probes can be combined to build a coherent physical picture.

I have experience with single-crystal and powder diffraction, inelastic neutron scattering, experiments under high pressure and low temperature, and infrared and THz spectroscopy.

Research

Research interests

Unconventional superconductivity

Iron-based systems, cuprates, nickelates, and the broader question of how magnetism, structure, and electronic correlations conspire to produce superconductivity.

Correlated magnetism

Exotic magnetism, low-dimensional systems, pressure-tuned phases, and new magnetic concepts that challenge simple localized-versus-itinerant pictures.

Spectroscopy and scattering

Neutron and synchrotron scattering, structural analysis, and infrared or THz probes of collective modes, spectral weight transfer, and phase competition.

PhD title

Study of the interplay of magnetism, structure, and electronic properties in the iron-based spin ladder BaFe₂S₃. The goal is to understand how low dimensionality, pressure, and competing interactions reshape the phase behavior of a material family closely linked to unconventional superconductivity.
